About This Service

My Korean Name is a free tool that generates a unique, meaningful Korean name for you — based on the initial sound of your name and your birth month. Each name is built from authentic Korean given-name syllables that real people use today, paired with Hanja characters (漢字) that carry centuries of cultural meaning.

How Korean Names Work

Korean names traditionally consist of a family name (성, seong) followed by a given name (이름, ireum). The given name is almost always two syllables long. Each syllable is written in Hangul (한글) — the Korean alphabet — but its deeper meaning comes from a corresponding Hanja character borrowed from Chinese.

Parents choose these characters carefully. Common themes include nature (flower, river, sky), virtues (wisdom, honesty, grace), and light (brightness, dawn, radiance). A name like 지혜 (智慧) means "wisdom and insight"; 민준 (敏俊) means "quick and talented."

How Your Name Is Generated

Your Korean name is composed of two syllables:

  • First syllable — drawn from a curated pool of authentic Korean name syllables that match the initial sound of your own name. For example, a name starting with "S" maps to syllables like 서, 소, or 솔 — all common in real Korean given names.
  • Second syllable — chosen based on your birth month. Each month offers three options with distinct Hanja meanings, reflecting the season and energy of that time of year. January brings characters meaning "radiant joy" or "pure light"; July offers "dawn light" or "the right path."

The combination is fully deterministic: the same name and birthday always produce the same Korean name, so you can share it confidently with friends.

Seasons and Personality

Korean culture deeply associates the season of one's birth with personality and destiny. Spring (봄) births are said to carry curiosity and warmth; summer (여름) brings bold energy; autumn (가을) brings quiet depth and wisdom; winter (겨울) is associated with calm resilience. Your result page includes a seasonal personality description rooted in this tradition.
